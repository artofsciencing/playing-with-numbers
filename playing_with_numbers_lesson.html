📝 Numbers in General Form

Every number has a secret skeleton — its general form!

🔬 What is General Form?

The general form expresses a number using its digits and place values.

2-digit number ab = 10a + b
3-digit number abc = 100a + 10b + c

Here a, b, c are individual digits (0–9), and a ≠ 0.

🔄 Reversal Magic

Flip a number's digits — and witness the hidden pattern!

🎩 2-Digit Reversal

Let a 2-digit number be ab = 10a + b. Its reverse is ba = 10b + a.

Sum = (10a+b) + (10b+a) = 11(a+b) → Always divisible by 11! ✓
Difference = (10a+b) − (10b+a) = 9(a−b) → Always divisible by 9! ✓

These results hold for any 2-digit number. That's the power of algebra!

🎩 3-Digit Reversal

Let abc = 100a + 10b + c. Reversed: cba = 100c + 10b + a.

Difference = 99(a − c) → Divisible by 9, 11, and 99!

Notice: the middle digit b cancels out completely!

🧩 The "Think of a Number" Trick

Here's a party trick based on this concept:

  1. Ask a friend to think of any 2-digit number
  2. Reverse the digits and subtract the smaller from the larger
  3. Tell them: "Your answer is divisible by 9!"

You'll always be right — because 9(a−b) is always a multiple of 9!

🔐 Cryptarithmetic

Each letter hides a digit. Your job: decode the puzzle!

📖 The Rules

  • Each letter represents a unique digit (0–9)
  • The first digit of any number cannot be 0
  • Use trial-and-error + logic to narrow down possibilities
  • Always verify your answer by substituting back in

🧩 Solved Example

 A B
+ B A
= 1 5 4

Step 1: AB = 10A+B, BA = 10B+A

Step 2: Sum = 11A + 11B = 11(A+B) = 154

11(A+B) = 154 → A+B = 14

Step 3: Possible pairs (A,B): (5,9), (6,8), (7,7), (8,6), (9,5)

Step 4: Any pair works! e.g. A=5, B=9 → 59+95=154 ✓
